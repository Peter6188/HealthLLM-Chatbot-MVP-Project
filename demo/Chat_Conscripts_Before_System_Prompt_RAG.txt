Migraines are typically characterized by:1. Recurring, severe headaches on one or both sides of the head2. Sensitivity to light (photophobia) and sound (hyperacusis)3. Nausea and vomiting (common in more severe cases)However, there are other conditions that can cause similar symptoms. For example:1. Tension headache: Can be caused by muscle tension, stress, or fatigue.2. Sinus headache: Related to sinusitis or allergic reactions.3. Cluster headache: A rare condition characterized by recurring headaches on one side of the head.To help determine if you’re experiencing a migraine, consider these additional factors:1. Frequency and duration of your headaches2. Any other symptoms, such as aura (visual disturbances), numbness, tingling, or changes in taste/smell3.
